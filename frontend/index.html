The Problem We're Solving

💻

No Computer at Home

Over 80% of Ethiopian high school students don't have a personal computer. When teachers assign coding homework, students have nowhere to practice.

📄

Code Written on Paper

Without a way to run code, programming becomes theoretical. Students memorize syntax instead of developing real problem-solving skills.

⏱️

Lab Time is Scarce

School computer labs are shared by hundreds of students. Each student gets just 1–2 hours per week — not nearly enough to learn by doing.
